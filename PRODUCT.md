# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences: (1) companies and independent founders scouting an AI consultant for NLP or product work; (2) recruiters and HR teams evaluating senior technical profiles. Both arrive expecting a fast read: can this person solve the problem? They scan for credibility, then contact.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and professional landing page for Tommaso Iaquinta, AI Consultant and NLP expert with a PhD in Computer Engineering and a background in Physics. The site's job is to make visiting companies or recruiters confident enough to get in touch. Success = a contact request or CV download.

## Brand Personality

Practical, precise, direct. The voice of someone who has shipped real systems and taught complex topics — no hype, no self-promotion theatre. Three words: **Rigorous. Grounded. Legible.**

## Anti-references

- Generic corporate portfolio templates (LinkedIn-style, stock photos, buzzwords)
- Startup AI hype: "transform your business with the power of AI", excessive animations, hero metrics
- Overly creative or artistic designer-portfolio aesthetics — this is a technical expert, not a visual artist

## Design Principles

1. **Competence speaks plainly.** Copy names specific technologies, concrete outcomes, and real project names. No claims that could apply to anyone.
2. **Dark because it earns it.** The aesthetic references (Vercel, Raycast, Warp) use dark not for style but because their users live in dark environments. A developer-adjacent AI consultant shares that context.
3. **Speed over ceremony.** Everything above the fold should answer "who is this and why should I care?" in under five seconds. Navigation and structure serve scanning, not storytelling.
4. **Bilingual without friction.** EN and IT are peers; the switch should feel native, not like an afterthought bolted on.
5. **Contact is the conversion.** Every section should reduce friction toward getting in touch or downloading the CV — not toward more content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG level required. Aim for sensible defaults: legible contrast on dark backgrounds, keyboard-navigable interactive elements, meaningful link text.
